Its title is 'Performance benchmark entre SQL Server y MongoDB: un acercamiento con enfoque en el modelo de datos' or something like 'Performance benchmark between SQL Server and MongoDB: an approach focused on the data model'.

Here is the abstract

This research consists in performing a comparison of the performance of relational and document-oriented databases. To remove the generic aspect from the previous sentence, it must be remembered that the same title of the research focuses on the data model, that is, it tries to go a step beyond that of a simple benchmarking using a single table.

The database engines to be used are two: SQL Server as the relational implementation and MongoDB on behalf of the document-oriented model. The databases used are four, two relational and two documentoriented. The original databases are derived from two application domains. One domain is naturally relational and the other is naturally non-relational. The idea of the investigation is to use the two original databases and create two databases derived in the other model, thus obtaining the four databases to be used.

Through the execution of tests, it will be tried to establish the performance for each one of the study variables: CPU, RAM and response time. Once analyzed the results, it will be tried to establish if the application domains had better results in their natural implementations or not.

This research will allow the reader to have an overview of the functioning of the relational databases, the document-oriented database, and the techniques for capture and analysis of data involved in a benchmarking.
